About Heart Disease

What is Heart Disease?

Heart disease refers to various types of conditions that affect the heart or blood vessels. It's the leading cause of death globally. The term "heart disease" encompasses several conditions, including coronary artery disease, arrhythmias, heart defects, and more.

Common Symptoms

  • Chest pain or discomfort (angina)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pain or numbness in the arms or legs
  • Dizziness or fatigue
  • Irregular heartbeat
  • Swelling in legs, ankles, or feet

Risk Factors

Age

The risk of heart disease increases as you get older, especially after age 45 for men and 55 for women.

Sex

Men are generally at greater risk of heart disease. However, women's risk increases after menopause.

Family History

A family history of heart disease increases your risk, especially if a parent developed it at an early age.

Blood Pressure

High blood pressure can damage your arteries and heart, increasing your risk of heart disease.

Cholesterol Levels

High levels of cholesterol in your blood can increase the risk of plaque formation in your arteries.

Diabetes

Diabetes significantly increases your risk of developing heart disease.

Prevention

Healthy Diet

Eat a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Limit salt, sugar, and unhealthy fats.

Regular Exercise

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous exercise each week.

Avoid Smoking

Smoking is a major risk factor for heart disease. If you smoke, quit; if you don't, don't start.

About Our Prediction Model
